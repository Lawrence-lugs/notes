CMOS 2.0: The Evolution of 3D In-Memory Computing

"CMOS 2.0" represents IMEC's vision for overcoming the "memory wall" in advanced logic. By disaggregating system-on-chips into distinct tiers—such as routing power through the backside and moving memory directly on top of compute logic—3D heterogeneous integration becomes the new Moore's Law.

The eDRAM on Compute Imperative

A major focus over the past decade has been addressing the leakage and area constraints of SRAM for Analog In-Memory Computing (AiMC). IMEC has pioneered Monolithic 3D (M3D) integration, prominently featuring IGZO (Indium Gallium Zinc Oxide) based eDRAM. Because IGZO can be deposited at low temperatures (BEOL compatible), dense eDRAM memory arrays can be fabricated directly on top of standard silicon CMOS analog compute macros without damaging them, enabling massive bandwidth for AI weights.
